# HQ VISUAL BIBLE: The Brand House
*Status: PRODUCTION-READY | Vector-Lock Enabled*

## 0. THE CORE IDENTITY
**The Brand House** is the Tier 1 elite. The "fine dining" of the franchise world. They serve prime cuts to the Board and the wealthy citizens of Lumenridge. They project an image of rugged luxury and hospitality, but their kitchens operate on militaristic brigade systems. They are the gatekeepers of status.

---

## 1. THE CREW ROSTER & COLOR LOCKS
*To be enforced strictly in all AI prompting and vector color-flattening.*

### The Bloomin' Cartel (Archetype: Outback Steakhouse)
* **The Vibe:** "No Rules, Just Right" is their legal doctrine. They project an invented, rugged mythology (Australian outback) to justify their brutal monopoly on high-margin meats and imported spices.
* **Vector Color Lock:** **Mahogany & Hunter Green**
* **Uniforms:** Black chef coats in the BOH; FOH wears rugged bush-shirts with excessive, mandatory flair that signifies rank and kill-count.
* **Medallion Icon:** Multiple concentric rings resembling a cross-sectioned blooming onion.

### The Roadhouse Riders (Archetype: Texas Roadhouse)
* **The Vibe:** Blue-collar aspiration. They are loud, rowdy, and heavily armed. They enforce loyalty through mandatory line-dancing rituals and control the peanut-dust-choked supply routes.
* **Vector Color Lock:** **Barn Red & Saddle Brown**
* **Uniforms:** Denim, boots, and massive belt buckles that double as brass knuckles. BOH is soaked in meat sweat and steak seasoning.
* **Medallion Icon:** A stylized, branded crossway 'T'.

### The Fallen Stars (Archetype: Lone Star Steakhouse)
* **The Vibe:** The faction in decline. They once ruled the steakhouse tier but lost the turf war to the Bloomin' Cartel. Now they operate as desperate mercenaries and black-market meat smugglers.
* **Vector Color Lock:** **Faded Denim & Tarnished Silver**
* **Uniforms:** Ragged remnants of their former glory. Cracked leather vests, tarnished star badges.
* **Medallion Icon:** A single, five-pointed star.

---

## 2. INTERNAL POLITICS (The Civil Wars)

* **The FOH / BOH Divide:** The primary conflict within The Brand House isn't between crews, but between classes. The Front-of-House (FOH) are the charismatic, high-earning diplomats. The Back-of-House (BOH) are the exhausted, substance-dependent grunts running the massive grills. The tension is always one bad shift away from a riot.
* **The Meat Monopoly:** The Bloomin' Cartel holds the dominant contract with Ghost Oil for prime cuts. The Roadhouse Riders constantly attempt to hijack these shipments to feed their massive volume needs.

---

## 3. EXTERNAL DIPLOMACY (The Street Matrix)

* **Active Alliance — Ghost Oil Refineries:** The Brand House is the only faction that can afford Ghost Oil's Tier-1 premium cuts. The executives of Ghost Oil dine exclusively at Brand House tables.
* **Kill-on-Sight — The Fresh Syndicate:** The Brand House views the "clean eating" agenda of the Fresh Syndicate as a direct threat to their high-calorie, meat-heavy empire.
* **Tense Neutrality — The Coastal Fryers (Seafood):** They share the "Casual Fine Dining" tier, occasionally collaborating on "Surf and Turf" treaties to price-gouge the middle class, but they fundamentally distrust each other's supply chains.

---

## 4. VISUAL & ARCHITECTURAL ANCHORS
*For use in all concept art and panel generation.*

* **Lighting:** Very dim, atmospheric. Heavy reliance on warm amber pendant lights over individual booths, leaving the aisles in shadow. The kitchen, by contrast, is a blindingly bright, stainless-steel operating room.
* **Atmosphere:** The smell of charred meat, blooming onions, and expensive cigars. A deceptive warmth that hides the ruthless financial extraction taking place.
* **Architecture:** Wood paneling, exposed brick, dark leather booths. Corridors are narrow and labyrinthine, designed to keep the BOH reality completely hidden from the FOH dining room.
* **Vector Note:** The contrast is key here. The FOH should be rendered in deep, rich, warm shadows (mahogany, amber). The BOH should be stark, cold, and frantic (ice blue, stainless steel, blinding white). The vector lines should be thicker in the shadows to emphasize the noir elements.
